**Core观点 (Core Points):**  
- The future battlefield will be high-tempo, complex, and contested across air, land, sea, space, and cyber domains, requiring resilient, adaptive, and networked combat air systems.  
- FCAS must be designed from a System-of-Systems (SoS) perspective, integrating platforms, sensors, weapons, command & control, and support systems into a cohesive force.  
- Key capabilities include superior situational awareness, secure communications, autonomy, AI-driven decision support, and covert operations.  
- Saab's approach is evolutionary, leveraging the Gripen platform while exploring new manned and unmanned configurations—including VLO UCAVs and expendable drones—to meet diverse mission needs.  
- Technology trends like additive manufacturing, quantum computing, energy weapons, and AI present both opportunities and threats that must be actively monitored and exploited.  
- Operational scenarios span territorial defense, international crisis response, cyber/space operations, and humanitarian missions, demanding flexible, scalable solutions.  
- Early concept validation and demonstration are critical to de-risk future development and inform strategic investment decisions.
